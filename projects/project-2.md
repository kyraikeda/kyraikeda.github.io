<h2>DESCRIPTION</h2>
This project asked to craft a magazine cover about a theme or subject of my choosing. To successfully complete the project, I had to create a magazine cover that used all or most of these features: masthead, dateline, main image, model credit, cover lines, main cover line, left third, bar code, and selling line. I also had to keep in mind the CRAP principles and other theories and elements of design to ensure that my magazine cover looked professional and well-made. 

<h2>PROCESS</h2>
I started out by picking my main image so that I can position the title, cover lines, and all the extras around it. After positioning the main image, I created a text layer for “Bloom” in light pink and used a clipping mask to allow part of the top flower to pop out. Then, I duplicated “Bloom”, recolored it to a dark red/pink, sent it to the back of the first layer, and used a clipping mask to let the whole flower be seen. Next, I added the dateline and cover lines and made sure to keep them either right aligned or left aligned. To make the barcode, I googled a picture of a <a href="https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Example_barcode.svg">barcode</a> that is labeled for reuse with modification and created a white rectangle to put behind it. I decided to crop the top part of the barcode because I liked the appearance of a thin barcode. To make the “Free seeds inside” sticker, I first created a dark, muted pink circle without a stroke and set the opacity to 89%. Then I created another circle with a white stroke and no fill, and I transformed it so it’s smaller than the first circle and fits inside. For a final touch, I played with the curves adjustment layer and gradient maps to add contrast and a tint of color to the main image. 

<h2>COMPOSITION + CONCEPT</h2>
For my layout, I loosely followed the rule of thirds and let the flowers guide the eye towards the content. Also, my main cover line relates to the main image since it’s of a flower called white flax. I did not include a model credit because it's unnecessary since the main image is of flowers. I decided not to use a super colorful and vibrant image because I wanted to make it look elegant and let the audience focus more on the form of the flowers. I wanted to make this magazine appeal more to the teens and young adults crowd by staying away from the generic gardening magazine look. I crushed the blacks in the photo to make it appear vintage and appeal to my intended audience more. I added grain to the photo too so that there would be some texture to the cover since all the text and shapes are smooth. To create some unity and variation in the cover, I made the masthead, border, and sticker different shades of pink. I think the pink and yellow-green color scheme contrasted nicely since they’re almost complements of each other.
